# Forging of [[Nightfall, the Hand that Feeds]]
## Process
The process to forge Nightfall requires mercury to be imbued with vampire blood and alchemically transmuted to a solid ingot. Then the ingot will need to be  imbued with immense magical force, as the Primordial Shard is then hammered into the metal. This task cannot be done by a machine, there needs to be a will behind it or the shard won't stick. It'll just devour the power and remain as it is.

Then, before it has a moment to consolidate itself, the weapon must be shaped. Immense heat and might and skill, as all great blades require.

The sheath requires soft obsidian on the interior. Kanan is baffled to think such a substance exists, but Grelchar tells him it's real and that he can get it here. The dwarves have been known to tan leather from stone before, so Kanan just goes with it.

The guard will be crafted of carved gold with a gradient purity, becoming more impure towards the hilt to channel the powers of the weapon toward the will of the wielder.

The most important part of the process is the lock and chain. It will need to be a holy implement, forged of platinum and each rung quenched in holy oils so that the monstrous will and power of the weapon can be subdued.

## Forging Nightfall Challenge
Forging Nightfall is a mythical task, requiring the magical and physical might of the heroes of old, and the will to survive the ordeal. *Forcing* primordial essence of any nature to behave how you desire is a feat that may even slay one who attempts it.

### Refining the Sanguine Mercury
Once the sanguine mercury ingot is prepared, arcane power will need to be channeled into the metal while the Primordial Shard is hammered into it. Spellcasters can channel a spell slot of third level or higher into the metal to charge the ingot. Then the Primordial Shard must be infused into the charged metal. As an action, make a Strength (Smith's Tools) check to hammer the essence into the ingot. The DC of the check is 40 minus the total levels of spells imbued within the metal. Success or fail, the spell levels are consumed.

Kanan may use his flame rite upon the hammer to add one roll of his hemocraft die to the Strength (Smith's Tools) check, as the flame of his soul is irreversibly linked to the Primordial Essence.

Failing the strength check by 5 or more causes the person attempting to refine the Sanguine Mercury to have their strength score reduced to 5 for 2d4 days, if it isn't 5 or lower already. Every day spent doing nothing that would end a long rest reduces the amount of recovery time required by 2 days.

### Shaping Nightfall's Teeth
With the material imbued, it will need to be shaped. Only the fires of the Steinwalde Heart-Kiln have the heat required to soften the metal now. Once the ingot is made nearly white-hot, a DC 22 Constitution (Smith's Tools) check must be succeeded to forge the weapon into the correct shape. Each attempt takes an hour, and upon completion of the hour, success or fail, the smith attempting to forge it suffers three levels of exhaustion if they are not resistant to fire damage, two levels of exhaustion if they are resistant to fire damage, and one level of exhaustion if they are immune to fire damage.

### Sharpening Hunger's Appetite
With the blade shaped, the cutting edge and the will of Nightfall must be refined. Using an Adamantine grindstone to hone the blade is but one part of the task. As its appetite grows, Nightfall will seek sustenance wherever it can find it. The final task in the forging of the weapon requires one to safeguard their life essence with their own till. As the weapon is sharpened, cuts and lacerations appear on the body of the craftsman working on the blade. Make three DC 17 Wisdom Saving Throws as your will pushes against the Primordial hunger of the weapon. You suffer necrotic damage equal to one roll of all of your hit dice on a failure, and half as much on a success. Your hit points cannot be restored without forfeiting your progress on sharpening the blade. Should this damage reduce you to zero hit points, you die.

Once the third saving throw is survived, the blade of [[Nightfall, the Hand that Feeds]] is complete.
